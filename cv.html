Publications and manuscripts

Language as a Wave Phenomenon: Semantic Phase Locking and Interference in Neural Networks
ICML 2026 Main Track | Alper Yıldırım, İbrahim Yücedağ

Shows that neural networks can use phase as a primary computational variable rather than as a residual feature of activations. Introduces a complex-valued, FFT-based sequence model where semantic structure is represented through phase relationships and spectral interference, suggesting a route toward efficient architectures that are more interpretable by design.

The Geometric Inductive Bias of Grokking: Bypassing Phase Transitions via Architectural Topology
Under review | Sole author

Studies how geometric constraints on residual streams affect memorization and generalization dynamics. Spherical L2 topology reduces grokking onset by about 25x, while controls show the effect is task-structure specific rather than generic regularization.

Superposition Is Not Necessary: Mechanistic Interpretability of Transformer Representations for Time Series
Under review | Sole author

Uses sparse autoencoders and causal interventions to study PatchTST representations. Finds no strong evidence of superposition in the tested forecasting representations, offering a mechanistic explanation for why simple linear models remain competitive.

HAMON: A Passive Optical Forecasting Core for Long-Horizon Time Series
In preparation | Sole author

Passive diffractive optical architecture for forecasting with no digital forecasting head. Uses phase encoding, diffractive propagation, and differential intensity readout.
