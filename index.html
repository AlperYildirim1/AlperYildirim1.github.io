Research

Language as a Wave Phenomenon: Semantic Phase Locking and Interference in Neural Networks
ICML 2026 Alper Yıldırım, İbrahim Yücedağ PDF ICML

PRISM replaces attention with complex-valued spectral filtering (FFT→H→IFFT). Interventional ablations show phase is not a byproduct but the network's preferred information carrier: phase-only filters retain >95% of performance, while removing phase is catastrophic. This holds even under phase-hostile conditions (CReLU + LayerNorm), demonstrating that phase-based computation emerges from the spectral topology itself.

The Geometric Inductive Bias of Grokking: Bypassing Phase Transitions via Architectural Topology
Under Review Alper Yıldırım

Constraining Transformer residual streams to a spherical topology eliminates the grokking delay entirely on modular arithmetic, but fails completely on non-commutative S5 permutation composition. This differential outcome shows the acceleration reflects task-specific geometric alignment, not generic regularization.

Superposition Is Not Necessary: A Mechanistic Interpretability Analysis of Transformer Representations for Time Series Forecasting
Under Review Alper Yıldırım

Sparse autoencoder analysis of PatchTST reveals no evidence of strong superposition in its feed-forward representations. Dictionary expansion from 0.5× to 4.0× yields 0.214% average performance change, and causal interventions on dominant latents produce minimal forecast perturbation, offering a mechanistic explanation for why simple linear models remain competitive.

HAMON: A Passive Optical Forecasting Core for Long-Horizon Time Series
In Preparation Alper Yıldırım

Historical context is encoded on an optical aperture, future positions are left dark, and cascaded trainable phase masks shape diffraction into the forecast region. No digital forecasting head. Competitive on several benchmarks with phase-scrambling ablations confirming the core uses temporal phase structure. Validated with TorchOptics as an independent cross-simulator check.
